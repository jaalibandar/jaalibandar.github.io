Generative Art: What & Why?

Although generative art has been around since the 1960s, explorations have often been limited to traditional algorithms to produce a volume of work with an additional curatorial layer. As modern technology keeps evolving at an accelerated pace, the ways and tools of practicing generative art has grown to accommodate a modest audience. Generative tech stands out to be creative but pretty underused despite its limitless potential as a medium to scale.

In this modern world, funding rapidly developing organizations has become the norm and scalability has become a keyword for success. Generative art provides artists with just that - the ability to scale up one's creative expression if only the artist is bold enough to go beyond the traditional arts practice. 

What is generative art? 


The process of creating certain instructions and automating it to create variations by the inherent design of the instructions provided.
Remember the conceptual artist 'Sol Lewitt'? 

There have been all forms of generative art experiments. Ranging from posters, music, videos and experiences. For developers it is much easy to get into generative art vs designers/artists from non tech backgrounds. 

However the new age low-code tools have greatly improved accessibility to generative technologies over the past two decades. With generative art the possibilities are endless constrained only by one's own imagination and one's skill in terms of the chosen medium of creation. As NFTs become a thing, it gives generative art a new life.

Who would have thought that a JPEG could actually be a thing - doesn't change the fact the people still debate the validity of NFTs as a concept.

Its pretty hard - to wrap one's head around the fact that one can be the 'owner' of a transparent image - which sounds pretty useless - however the beauty lies in the addressability and accountability of things. Something that has long been missing from our tech stacks by design.

It probably scares the shit out of people and well it should. 

Medium is the message.

Curatorial direction is a key deterministic factor in the practice of generative art. Why does one use certain methods to create something is sometimes more important than the 'what' has one finally created. Its like the painter trying to figure out what to paint before one actually puts down the brush against the canvas. Although for some it may be a process, a journey, a practice of creating something with just a few lines of code. 

Why generative art? 

Generative art accommodates more than just the artwork. Its not just an image. Its many images - a hundred, a thousand or a million by design. The power within is immense whether one leverages it to simulate natural systems or to layer hand drawn images. 

AI art brings in a new dimension by minifying the generative experience, limiting the creativity of the user to a single prompt. On one hand it solves the problem of scale by allowing for changes to the inherent algorithmic design. 

On the other hand changes to some other part of the system design can result in something else entirely.

It still feels like the early days of an artform although having been around for decades as artists find newer ways to use generative art to aid their practices. A diverse yet beautiful medium that evolves every passing day.
